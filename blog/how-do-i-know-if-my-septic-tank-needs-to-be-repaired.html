How do I know if my septic tank needs to be repaired?

If you’ve bought a home with a septic tank, you know it’ll be something you’ll have to deal with eventually. What does a septic tank do?

A septic tank is used to treat wastewater from a home, breaking it down as it travels through the system. This is known as an anaerobic process, which means it occurs without oxygen. This is because there is no free oxygen in a septic tank. It’s essentially a giant vat of sewage.The sewage travels through a series of pipes that lead to the tank, which is filled with a special type of bacteria that breaks down waste. After the tank is full, the waste is pumped out of the house and into the local soil. There are two types of bacteria used in a septic tank: aerobic and anaerobic. Aerobic bacteria require fresh oxygen, which is why it’s recommended to have aeration systems in the tank.Anaerobic bacteria thrive in the absence of oxygen. They can break down waste materials in a variety of conditions, including in a tank with no oxygen.

How to tell if your septic tank needs repair or replacement

It’s a good idea to have a professional come out and check your septic tank before you’re ready to move in. They can test the tank to see how much wastewater it is able to process and how quickly it breaks down the waste. If the tank is working properly, it should be able to break down waste within a few days. If it takes longer than a week for the waste to be broken down, it’s time to call a contractor.A septic tank that isn’t working as it should is a hazard to anyone living in your house. If you have guests over and the tank is full, it could start to overflow. This could lead to a costly cleanup and cause damage to your property. If you’ve noticed that your tank isn’t working as well as it should, it’s a good idea to have it checked before you move in.
