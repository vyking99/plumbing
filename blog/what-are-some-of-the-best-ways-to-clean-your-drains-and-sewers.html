What are some of the best ways to clean your drains and sewers?

Regularly cleaning your drains and sewers is important for the health of your home and its occupants. Without regular maintenance, drains and sewer pipes can become clogged and back up, which is not only inconvenient but can also be dangerous to your home. To avoid this and keep your drains looking their best, there are a number of different methods you can use to clean them. Each method has its own pros and cons so it’s important to understand which method might work best for you in your specific circumstances. If you’re not sure how to clean your drains and sewers, we have some useful tips on how to get started – as well as advice on how to choose the best cleaning method for your home situation.

Hydro Jetting for Drains and Sewers

Hydro-jetting is a method whereby water is pumped through a high-pressure hose to clean out your drains and sewer pipes. This method is particularly effective for clearing large clogs and debris from drains and sewer pipes.However, it’s important to note that hydro-jetting isn’t suitable for all types of drains. For example, it can be an effective way to clear a kitchen or bathroom sink’s trap, but it won’t be as effective for a sewer line. Hydro-jetting is often recommended for large-scale commercial or industrial buildings that have multiple drains and sewers. It can also be useful for homes with high-maintenance landscaping.Hydro-jetting is best for drains and sewers that have been clogged for a long time, or if there’s been a large amount of build-up in the pipes. The water pressure used during hydro-jetting can also help break up and loosen hard-to-remove build-ups.

Diverting Flow Through a Manhole

A diverter is a tool that allows you to divert the flow of water into a sewer main. This can be useful for cleaning out a sewer line, but keep in mind that this method won’t be effective for cleaning smaller drains and sewers.If you have a large clog in your drains and sewers, diverting the flow of water into a sewer main may be an effective way to clear out the blockage. However, this method may not be appropriate for smaller drains. You should always use the lowest level of plumbing as a diverter, otherwise the flow of water could cause damage and flooding in your home.A diverter can be helpful when you have a large-scale clog or a large amount of debris in your drains and sewers. It can also be useful for large-scale commercial and industrial buildings with multiple drains and sewers. A diverter can be installed as a temporary solution until you can access the sewer main and clear the clog.

Chemical Cleaning for Drains and Sewers

Chemical cleaning is a method whereby a cleaning solution is pumped through the drains and sewers to break up any build-up. This method can be useful for cleaning small drains and sewers that are prone to build-up.If you have a large clog in your drains and sewers, chemical cleaning can be an effective way to clear the blockage. This method is often used for commercial and industrial buildings that have multiple drains and sewers.Chemical cleaning is best for drains and sewers that have been clogged for a long time, or if there’s been a large amount of build-up in the pipes. The cleaning solution used during chemical cleaning can also help break up and loosen hard-to-remove build-ups.However, it’s important to note that chemical cleaning is not suitable for all types of drains. In particular, it is not recommended for kitchen sinks and bathroom sinks. Chemical cleaning is safe to use, but you should always read and follow the instructions on the cleaning product packaging.
