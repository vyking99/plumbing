What is sewer pipe lining?

When your sewer pipes have been in place for many years, they may have suffered from wear and tear, corrosion and abuse from tree roots or other damage. This can make them more prone to leaks and backups, which can be inconvenient and even dangerous. Sewer pipe lining is one of the most effective long-term solutions for repairing and resurfacing your sewer pipes. It’s a cost-effective alternative to sewer line replacement, with a typical project costing anywhere from $10,000 to $30,000. It’s also an excellent option for those who don’t have the budget to replace their sewer pipes altogether; by lining the existing pipes, you can extend their lifespan by another 20 to 50 years.

Why is sewer pipe lining important?

When your sewer pipes are old and have been damaged, they'll need to be replaced eventually. However, sewer line replacement can be a costly and disruptive process. It involves digging up the pipes and installing new, longer ones in their place, making sure you don't have a back-up in the meantime. Sewer pipe lining, on the other hand, doesn't require you to dig up your pipes or otherwise disrupt your home. It's not a permanent solution like a sewer line replacement, but it does extend the life of your pipes by up to 50 years. This can add up to significant savings in the long run, as you'll need to replace your sewer pipes even sooner if you're planning on selling your home in the near future.

How does sewer pipe lining work?

Sewer pipe lining is a bit different from traditional pipe repair, but the basic principles are the same. It involves coating the inside of your sewer pipes with a protective liner, which is usually made of polyethylene or some similar material. Once the liner is in place, it begins to break down, eventually forming a new, watertight pipe within the old, damaged one. Depending on the severity of the damage to your pipes, the process could take anywhere from one day to several weeks. Once the lining is in place and the sewage has been diverted to another pipe, you can go ahead and repair the damage in the old pipes.

Why is pipe lining more effective than repair?

Unlike repair, pipe lining doesn't require you to dig up the pipes or otherwise disrupt your home. It also has a higher success rate, with studies showing that it can extend the life of your pipes by up to 50 years. The most important reason for using pipe lining instead of repair is that it's less disruptive. When you repair your pipes, you have to divert the flow of water in the meantime. This can cause backups in your home and other issues that could be inconvenient and even dangerous. Pipe lining, on the other hand, doesn't require you to divert the flow of water. This makes it a safer and less disruptive option for both the homeowner and the occupants.

Wrapping up

Pipe lining is a less disruptive and more effective solution than repair, making it the ideal option for dealing with the damage that your sewer pipes have suffered over the years. It also has the added benefit of being less expensive than full-blown replacement. However, it's important to remember that it's not a permanent solution. The pipes will eventually need to be replaced, so you still need to keep an eye out for signs of wear and tear and take action as soon as possible.
