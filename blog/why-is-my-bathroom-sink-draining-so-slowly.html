Why is my bathroom sink draining so slowly?

The slow flow of water in your bathroom sink can be frustrating and unnerving. One of the reasons why this happens is because the water is constantly being drawn down by the force of gravity. You might think that a slow flow of water in your bathroom sink is normal, but it’s not! It can be a real nuisance if you can’t wash your hands easily because they’ve become so dirty. There are several reasons why your bathroom sink might be draining slowly. You will find that some of these reasons are more common than others, but they can all be resolved with some simple fixes. You may need a plumber if there is a clog in your pipes or if your water pressure is too low for your sink to work properly. Here are some common reasons why your bathroom sink slows down or stops completely:

Your water pressure is too low.

The water pressure in your house is controlled by the water department. If your water pressure is too low, it will cause your bathroom sink to run slowly. You might think that you can increase the pressure by turning up the faucet or using a water pressure regulator, but that will do you no good. The problem is that the water department controls the water pressure, not you. You can try and contact the water department to ask for a higher pressure, but you might have to pay for it. Another option is to put a water pressure regulator on the incoming pipe. Most homes have a shut off valve before the water enters the house. If you can shut off the water at that point, it will prevent the water department from turning up the pressure for too high a flow rate.

Your sink is clogged.

The most common reason that your bathroom sink drains slowly is that it is clogged. Most sinks have a trap beneath them that prevents sewer gasses, debris, and other contaminants from entering the house. Over time, food particles, hair, and other debris can clog the trap and slow the flow of water in the pipes. This is an easy problem to fix, but you might have to call a plumber to unclog the trap. You can try a toilet plunger to clear the trap, but you might have to use a more powerful model. If the trap is clogged, the water will back up into the sink bowl and slow the flow of water.

Your pipes are old and need to be replaced.

If you notice that your bathroom sink is running slowly, the first thing you should do is inspect the pipes. If the pipes are old and rusted, they may have collapsed and will need to be replaced. If you have old copper pipes, the flow of water could be reduced even further if the pipes are leaking. You can try to repair the pipes, but they are likely in bad shape and will need to be replaced. If you are in the middle of a remodel and notice that your bathroom sink is slowing down, you might need to move up the date for the installation of new pipes. This will be a pricey fix, however, as you will likely need to replace the entire bathroom sink. If you live in an older home, you might have old asbestos cement pipes that are slowly crumbling and need to be replaced.

The trap is clogged or broken.

This is a small rubber piece that is located underneath the sink and prevents sewer gasses and debris from entering the house. The trap is connected to the drain pipe through a U-shaped piece of plastic called a trap arm. The trap arm and trap can be accessed through the trap opening. If the trap is clogged or broken, the water will back up into the sink and slow the flow of water. The best way to access the trap is to remove the trap arm. You can purchase a trap cleaning kit that will make this process much easier.

The pipes are so small that they are restricting flow.

If you have a very small bathroom, the pipes may be too small to accommodate the amount of water that is flowing through the system. In this case, you will need to increase the size of the pipes.
