How do I know if I need to replace my emergency hot water heater?

Keeping a home heated during cold weather can be an expensive proposition. In fact, it costs an average of $2000 more to heat your home in the winter than in the summer. One of the main reasons for this is the cost of heating hot water. In fact, a new tankless or hybrid electric water heater can save you as much as $800 per year in water and energy costs alone. But as expensive as they are upfront, they come with an equally significant price tag if they break down or become non-operational for any reason whatsoever. This is especially true for older, outdated heating systems like old-fashioned electric or tank-style water heaters.

Tank vs. Tankless

As the name implies, a tank water heater is essentially a large container that stores hot water ready to be used when needed. This means that when you turn on the hot water tap, the water coming out of the faucet will be slightly warmer than the surrounding air. A tank water heater is extremely reliable, but it can be costly to operate and can result in higher water bills due to the fact that you must continuously heat the water in the tank.A tankless water heater, on the other hand, is a small tank that is connected directly to the hot water line coming from your home’s plumbing system. This means that when you turn on the hot water tap, the water coming out of the faucet will be as hot as it was when it left the heater. This means that a tankless water heater requires only as much energy as necessary to heat the water that is actually used. It is estimated that a tankless water heater can save about $500 in energy costs per year over a conventional water heater.

Reasons to replace your old water heater

- Age: It’s generally recommended that you replace your water heater once it reaches a certain age. The average lifespan of a water heater is between 8 and 10 years. Some water heaters last longer than others depending on exactly where you live and how much you use water. If you’re in the process of selling your home, you may want to replace your water heater before the sale contract is finalized in order to avoid any unnecessary delays.- Cost: New water heaters cost more upfront, but the overall cost will be significantly less over time. Depending on your local utility rates and how much you’re currently paying for water, you may be able to save money by upgrading your water heater.- Location: Depending on where you live, your water heater may be exposed to extreme heat or extreme cold. In extremely cold climates, a water heater in a garage or other indoor location may not be able to keep the water at a temperature that is safe for human consumption.- Type: Depending on the type of water heater you currently have, you may be able to reduce the amount of money you spend by upgrading to a more energy efficient model.

Reasons to replace your old water heater

If you’re currently using a tank-style water heater, there are a few signs that may indicate it’s time for an upgrade. First and foremost, if your water heater is more than 10 years old, it probably won’t be able to produce hot water as efficiently as newer models. Secondly, if you’re noticing large fluctuations in your water bill (i.e. if the bill jumps up significantly after a particularly hot or cold day), it could be an indication that you need to replace your water heater.If you’re paying for gas and your water heater is electric, it may be a good idea to upgrade to a hybrid model. Hybrid water heaters combine the best aspects of both types of water heaters, making them more energy efficient and less costly to operate.

Tankless hot water heaters

If you’re in the market for a tankless hot water heater, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, tankless hot water heaters need to be connected directly to the hot water supply line in order to function properly. This means that you’ll need to replace your hot water heater’s supply pipe before installing the tankless hot water heater. You’ll also need to choose the right size for your home and your specific water needs. To determine the right size for your home, multiply the square footage of your house by 0.979. That will give you the size of your main hot water line. Next, add 2 inches to account for pipe insulation and other materials, resulting in a recommended size for your water heater.When choosing a tankless hot water heater, it’s important to consider the type of fuel source you’ll be using. There are several different types of tankless water heaters, each with their own pros and cons.

Hybrid water heaters

Hybrid water heaters combine the best aspects of both tank and tankless water heaters. They are more energy efficient than traditional tank water heaters and they are less expensive to operate than tankless water heaters. The main downside to hybrid water heaters is that they require an electric source of power and are not suitable for use in areas that are not connected to the grid. Hybrid water heaters are most commonly found in areas that are served by municipal water treatment facilities that use a water cooling system. Hybrid water heaters are ideal for households that are on a tight budget and need to stretch their water heating dollars as far as they can.

Conclusion

Water heating costs are often overlooked by home owners. However, inefficient water heating systems can cost thousands of dollars every year. With a new tankless or hybrid water heater, you can cut your water heating bill in half. Luckily, there are many different types of water heaters to choose from. When shopping for new water heaters, be sure to do your research and choose a model that best fits your needs. With the right type of water heater, you can cut down on your water heating bills and enjoy hot water for years to come.
